Youngest at Eidsvoll

  • Illustrated book
  • Published by Mangschou
  • Author: Ole Røsholdt
  • Layout / art direction: Karlsens
  • Design / titles: Åge Petterson
  • Charcoal & Photoshop illustrations

At a mere 17 years old, Thomas Konow was the youngest participant at Eidsvoll national assembly of 1814. The book gives his view of the events around the devising of the norwegian constitution. Thomas Konow participated in the ratification and signing of a constitution which would not give him the right to vote for another seven years.

Thomas Konow

Our introduction to Thomas. In the early spring of 1814 he is serving in the danish-norwegian marine.

Fabricius

Jens Schow Fabricius was Thomas Konow's commanding officer. The calling of a norwegian national assembly was not condoned by the danish king, but represented something of a rebellion against Norway being given to Sweden after the Napoleonic wars. Fabricius was the only high ranking officer answering the call, and Thomas Konow joined him.

The portrait is based on a painting by an unknown artist of the 19th century, but I needed to make him slightly younger.

Konow and Fabricius traveled several day by cart from the west coast towards Eidsvoll north east of Oslo.

Arrival at Eidsvoll.

Strongman

Participants from various social classes were attending the Eidsvoll assembly. One of the farmers famously showed of his strength by lifting a horse.

Venus on the wall

During the long, strainous hours of debate, sitting on a hard bench, Thomas bemones the fact he has to sit with his back towards a painting of the naked Venus.

Checks and balances

Inspired by the french and american constitutions, the norwegian constitution embraced the priciple of seperation of powers into executive, legislative and judicial branches. The king would retain the executive branch, but his power would be curtailed by the others.

Maps

Included in the book are several maps keeping up with the political situation on the continent. Especially the movements of Bernadote / Carl Johan, the would be king of Sweden, and later Norway, represented by the blue mounted pawn. The maps are made with Sketchup and Photoshop, and are meant to look a bit like gameboards.
