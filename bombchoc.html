Bomb Choclate

  • Illustrated book
  • Written by Bente Roestad
  • Digital drawings
  • 22 illustration spreads in total

Bomb Choclate is about Karin, and her experiences during the german occupation of Norway during the second World War. Through her eyes we get a view of what everyday life was like in Norway during those times. This is based on a true story, as Karin is the mother of author Bente Roestad.

The first chapter begins as Karin's family hears about the german invasion on the radio, early in the morning april 9th 1940.

(More illustrations below)

"Father, don't leave!"

In the 2nd chapter, Karin's father believes it is his duty to mobilize and fight the german invaders. His family pleads with him, and convinces him not to leave.

Karins Dream

The next night Karin has a dream, in which she meets her grandmother and her mother as a child.

The panic day

In the 4th chapter, Karin's father tells his family to leave Oslo, as he has heard the English are going to send bombers against the germans there, and it is not safe. When the anti-aircraft sirens go off, general panic breaks out.

Out in the country

In the 5th chapter, Karin, with her mother and sister, goes out in the country, near Kongsvinger, to meet her uncle Ole.

Back in town

In the 7th chapter, Karin is back in Oslo. German troops are marching in the streets, singing german songs. Karin decides that if she can outpace them, it's a sign that Norway and the allies will prevail in the end.

Bomb choclate

Every now and again, when the anti-aircraft sirens sound, the family has to seek shelter in a nearby sturdy cellar. They grab warm clothes, and Karin's mother gives her a piece of cooking choclate for comfort. This is where the book gets its name.

Leaflets in the night

In the 14th chapter, we learn that Karin's uncle is circulating illegal leaflets in the night. When accosted by german soldiers, he manages to deceive them by playing drunk.

Peace day, may 8th 1945

After five long years, the war is finally over with the german surrender to the allies. In Norway spontanous celebration breaks out in the streets. The illustration is based on a photography by an unknown photographer documenting the event (yes, the lorries were that crowded).
