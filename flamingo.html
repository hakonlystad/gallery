The last flamingo

  • Illustrated book
  • For Aurora Forlag
  • Author: Nina Hanssen
  • Pencil and Photoshop
  • 27 illustrations in total

June from Lofoten and Franz Flamingo from Lake Nakuru both discover that their local waters are polluted by plastic litter. After discovering a post card from Nakuru in bottle mail, June feels drawn to Nakuru, and when she finally gets to go, she joins with Franz and other animals in efforts to clean up the plastic garbage.

The driftwood plate on the cover is for the titles. It is wrapped in ghost nets to emphasize the theme, and give more connection to northern Norway.

June is a 12 year old girl from Lofoten in the north of Norway. Her big passion in life is photography.

After the discovery of a stranded whale with its belly full of plastic, June's environmental awareness is awakened, and she starts focusing on plastic littering.

June starts cleaning up empty bottles and other plastic litter on the shore. She comes across a bottle mail containing a postcard from Lake Nakuru, Kenya. She dreams about going there.

At lake Nakuru in Kenya, Franz Flamingo gets his foot stuck in plastic garbage, and when his flock migrates, he is left behind in the commotion.

Franz gets a helping horn from a friendly impala.

The impala offers Franz a ride on his back, as his foot is hurt, and the impala knows someone who can help. Maybe Franz was to shy to suggest flying might be better.

Friendly neighbors help patching up Franz' foot.

Franz and Impala are shocked to discover the extent of the plastic pollution.
