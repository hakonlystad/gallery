Captain Blich in trouble

  • interior book illustration
  • For Øyvind Flatnes
  • Digital painting

This illustration is for a book about the norwegian chamber-loading rifle of the 19th century. Captain Jens Christian Blich became an important proponent for its development after an incident in the short Norwegian-Swedish war of 1814. After a forward push in heavy fog and rain during night time, he found himself staring down the barrel of a swedish flintlock musket. The captain was only armed with a sabre, but the swede's musket failed to fire in the rain, and blich was able to defeat him in hand to hand combat.
